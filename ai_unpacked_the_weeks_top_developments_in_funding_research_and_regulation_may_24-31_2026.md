## Navigating the Regulatory Maze: Federal and State AI Legislation

The regulatory landscape for artificial intelligence continues to evolve, with both federal and state governments grappling with how to best govern this rapidly advancing technology. Federally, the policy environment remains largely unsettled. A highly anticipated White House cybersecurity order, intended to provide guidance on AI security, has seen delays, indicating the complexity of establishing comprehensive federal oversight [Source](https://www.transparencycoalition.ai/news/ai-legislative-update-may29-2026). Meanwhile, the Department of Health and Human Services (HHS) is actively pursuing its AI Risk and Equity Office (AERO) initiative, focusing on the ethical and equitable deployment of AI in healthcare [Source](https://www.mintz.com/insights-center/viewpoints/54941/2026-05-26-may-2026-health-policy-update-ai-washington-report).

At the state level, legislative efforts are gaining momentum, often serving as testing grounds for future federal approaches. New York's Responsible AI Standards and Ethics (RAISE) Act is a notable proposal, aiming to establish a framework for ethical AI development and deployment within the state [Source](https://www.transparencycoalition.ai/news/ai-legislative-update-may29-2026). Ohio is particularly active, with several bills addressing various facets of AI. These include proposals to regulate AI and algorithmic pricing, mitigate the risks of harmful AI models, and specifically govern the use of AI by health insurers [Source](https://www.transparencycoalition.ai/news/ai-legislative-update-may29-2026), [Source](https://www.mintz.com/insights-center/viewpoints/54941/2026-05-26-may-2026-health-policy-update-ai-washington-report).

Consumer protection remains a key focus across these legislative discussions. The proposed Consumer AI Notice Act (SB 317) seeks to mandate transparency, requiring companies to inform consumers when they are interacting with AI systems [Source](https://www.transparencycoalition.ai/news/ai-legislative-update-may29-2026). Additionally, student rights legislation, such as SB 416, is emerging to address the implications of AI in educational settings, ensuring fair and equitable treatment for students in an AI-driven academic environment [Source](https://www.transparencycoalition.ai/news/ai-legislative-update-may29-2026). These diverse legislative efforts underscore a growing recognition of AI's societal impact and the urgent need for thoughtful governance.
